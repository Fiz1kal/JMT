1. Yosemite Wilderness Permit — Primary JMT permit
Required to hike JMT starting from Happy Isles or Lyell Canyon. Apply at recreation.gov/permits/445859. Lottery opens 168 days (24 weeks) in advance, every Sunday for that week's start dates. $10 non-refundable lottery application fee + $10/person if awarded. 60% available by advance reservation; 40% available walk-up at 7am PDT, 7 days in advance online. Permit must specify "Donohue Pass" eligibility to exit Yosemite southbound on the JMT. Very competitive — apply immediately when the lottery opens for your target week.
2. Half Dome Permit — Required for cables
Required to ascend the subdome steps and Half Dome cables. Cables up approximately May 25 – October 15 each year. Pre-season lottery: March 1–31 at recreation.gov/permits/234652. Results announced mid-April. $10 non-refundable application fee + $10/person if awarded. Daily lottery: apply 2 days before your intended hike, between midnight and 4pm PT. ~18% success rate in pre-season lottery. Maximum 300 permits/day total (225 day hikers + 75 backpackers). Apply with a range of dates — weekdays have better odds. Start cables before 7am to avoid afternoon lightning and crowds.
3. Mt. Whitney — JMT southern terminus
The JMT wilderness permit covers hiking to the Whitney summit from the west (Crabtree Meadows) on your finish day. If camping near the Whitney summit, an additional Sequoia-Kings Canyon NP overnight permit is required. Confirm current requirements with the Inyo National Forest before your trip — regulations can change year to year.
4. Yosemite NP Entry Fee — Non-US residents
Standard $35/vehicle fee + approximately $100 additional for non-US residents in 2026. The America the Beautiful Annual Pass ($80 at recreation.gov) covers entry to all US national parks for 12 months — excellent value if you plan any other parks on this trip or return visits. Purchase before arrival.
5. Hungarian travelers: ESTA Visa Waiver
Hungary is a Visa Waiver Program country — no embassy visit or tourist visa needed. Apply online at esta.cbp.dhs.gov before departure. Cost: $21. Apply at least 72 hours before travel (apply as soon as you book flights). Valid 2 years / multiple trips. Allows stays up to 90 days in the USA per visit. Carry your passport and ESTA confirmation number when traveling.
6. California Fire Permit
Required for any open flame in California, including camp stoves — even in wilderness areas. Free, apply online at readyforwildfire.org before departure. Note that campfires are banned above certain elevations and during fire season — always check current restrictions. Camp stoves are generally permitted with the fire permit.
Application timeline for a July–August trip
March 1–31 (year of trip): Enter Half Dome pre-season lottery at recreation.gov · April (year of trip): Half Dome lottery results announced · ~24 weeks before start (spring, year of trip): Yosemite Wilderness Permit lottery opens — apply immediately when your week opens · 7 days before start: Check walk-up permit availability online · 2 days before your Half Dome day: Enter daily lottery as backup if pre-season unsuccessful · Before departure from Budapest: Book flights, purchase ESTA, arrange travel insurance, ship gear.
Target base weight: 5.5–7.3 kg (12–16 lbs) excluding food, water, and fuel. Bear canister adds ~900 g (2 lbs). Buy stove fuel in the USA — you cannot fly with it. Major gear can be purchased in Budapest or at REI San Francisco before the trail.

Where to buy in Europe (Budapest)
Budapest gear stores & online options
Intersport Hungary, Mammut brand dealers, and Decathlon (for budget items) carry most essentials. For ultralight/specialist gear: order from Patagonia, Arc'teryx, or Enlightened Equipment direct — all ship to Hungary. Zpacks ships internationally. Merino base layers from Icebreaker are widely available in Europe.

Do NOT bring: Stove fuel (isobutane canisters) on the plane — this is illegal. Buy at REI San Francisco SOMA location or at outdoor stores near Yosemite. Also do not buy a bear canister before checking with Kennedy Meadows / Triple Crown Outfitters — they have them on site.

Recommended trail apps & maps
FarOut (formerly Guthook) — Essential on-trail navigation
The most widely used JMT navigation app. Shows your GPS position relative to the trail even without cell signal. Download the JMT guidebook offline before your trip. Real-time water reports, campsite notes, and condition updates from other hikers are invaluable. Available on iOS and Android — $30–40 for the JMT guide.
Gaia GPS — Offline topo maps
Full USGS topo maps available offline. Download the Sierra Nevada tiles before departure. Excellent for cross-referencing with FarOut and for off-trail navigation near passes. Free basic version; $40/year for full offline access. Available on iOS and Android.
Tom Harrison Maps — Waterproof paper maps
The gold standard paper map series for the JMT. "John Muir Trail Map Pack" covers the entire trail on waterproof, tear-resistant paper. Highly recommended as a backup to your phone. Available at REI San Francisco and many outdoor retailers. ~$35 for the complete set.
CalTopo — Pre-trip route planning
Best browser-based tool for pre-trip planning. Measure distances, calculate elevation profiles, and export GPX routes to your phone or Garmin inReach. Free at caltopo.com — use it to study each pass before your trip.
